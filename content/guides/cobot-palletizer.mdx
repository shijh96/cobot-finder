## Critical Specs: What Matters for Palletizing Success

Not all cobots are equal for palletizing. Focus on these four specs:

### 1. Payload (Carrying Capacity)

Most palletizing boxes weigh **15-25 kg**. Your cobot's payload rating must exceed this — ideally by 30% for safety margin.

- **15-18 kg payload:** Light packaging (cosmetics, apparel, small electronics)
- **20-25 kg payload:** Standard boxes, mixed food products, light machinery parts
- **30 kg+ payload:** Heavy industrial goods, large beverage cases

<Callout variant="warning" title="The #1 spec mistake">
<p>Undersizing payload. A 10 kg cobot will technically lift a 12 kg box, but won't maintain speed or accuracy over thousands of cycles. Aim for <strong>20 kg+ payload</strong> if you're unsure.</p>
</Callout>

### 2. Reach (Arm Length)

Standard pallets are **1,200-1,400 mm tall** when fully stacked. Add box height (300-400 mm) and your cobot needs reach of at least **1,600 mm minimum** to access the top layer.

- **1,500 mm reach:** Good for waist-height pallets, shallow boxes
- **1,700 mm reach:** Standard for mixed pallet heights
- **1,900 mm reach:** Deep pallets or tall stacks

### 3. Cycle Time (Speed)

Most palletizing applications need **6-12 boxes/minute** to justify the investment. Example calculation: 5,000 boxes/shift / 480 minutes = 10.4 boxes/minute needed. Most cobots achieve 8-15 boxes/minute depending on pallet complexity.

A slower cobot (6 boxes/min) still works if you run double shifts — you're replacing 2 workers, not tripling throughput.

### 4. Gripper Type

Your gripper is **40% of palletizing success**. Two main types:

**Vacuum Grippers** (+$8k-12k) — Best for flat, consistent boxes. Gentle, adjustable pressure, works with varied orientations. Can't grip damaged or wet boxes.

**Mechanical Grippers** (+$5k-8k) — Best for irregular shapes and moisture-sensitive items. Reliable on wet/textured surfaces, faster cycle times. Requires corner detection.

<Callout variant="tip">
<p><strong>70% of successful cobot palletizers use vacuum grippers</strong> for speed and flexibility. Only choose mechanical grippers if your boxes are frequently wet, damaged, or irregular.</p>
</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between a "collaborative" palletizer and a regular industrial robot?** Cobots have built-in force limiting — if they hit a person, they stop immediately. Industrial robots require safety cages because they don't detect impacts. Cobots are safer, cheaper, and faster to deploy. Learn more about [what is a cobot](/guides/what-is-a-cobot).

**Can a cobot palletizer integrate with our existing conveyor system?** Yes. Vision-equipped cobots detect box positions on the conveyor and adjust grip accordingly. Without vision, boxes must feed into a fixed position (most common setup).

**How often does the gripper need maintenance?** Vacuum grippers need filter changes every 500-1,000 operating hours ($50-100 per change). Mechanical grippers need worn pad replacement every 1,000-2,000 hours ($200-400).

**What if our box sizes vary significantly?** Vision integration ($15k-20k) handles multiple box sizes automatically. Without vision, you're limited to 2-3 similar dimensions before manual reconfiguration.

**Is programmer experience required?** No. UR Palletizing URCap and Pally use drag-and-drop interfaces. Even non-technical operators can adjust patterns. For complex logic, you'll want a cobot integrator ($150-200/hour for customization).

**What's the difference between this and [cobots in manufacturing](/guides/cobots-in-manufacturing) generally?** Palletizing is just one manufacturing use case. Cobots also handle assembly, pick-and-place, [welding](/guides/cobots-for-welding), and material handling. This guide focuses specifically on end-of-line box stacking.

**What happens if the cobot breaks during production?** Service calls cost $1,500-$3,000 and take 1-2 days. Maintenance contracts ($3k-5k/year) include faster response times (same-day) and spare parts inventory.

## The Bottom Line: Is a Cobot Palletizer Right for Your Operation?

**A cobot palletizer makes financial sense if:**
- You're palletizing 500-5,000 boxes per shift
- Your current labor spend exceeds $120k/year for this function
- You need deployment in under 6 months
- Your product mix is moderate (2-5 different box types)
- You have 3-4m x 2m of available floor space

**Consider traditional robots or manual labor if:**
- You're palletizing 20,000+ identical boxes per shift (industrial robot wins on per-unit cost)
- Your boxes weigh more than 30 kg (beyond cobot capacity)
- Your product variety requires complete line redesigns monthly
- You lack budget for software integration ($15k+)

For the vast majority of mid-market [manufacturing](/guides/cobots-in-manufacturing) and food companies, cobot palletizers represent the sweet spot: faster ROI than industrial automation, lower injury risk than manual labor, and flexibility to adapt as your business grows. With payback periods under 9 months and 3-year ROIs exceeding 300%, they're one of the highest-returning automation investments available today.
